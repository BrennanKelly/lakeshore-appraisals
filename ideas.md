## Idea A: "Great Lakes Nautical Cartography"

**Design Movement:** Mid-century cartographic modernism meets coastal professionalism

**Core Principles:**
1. Map-inspired grid lines and coordinate typography as structural motifs
2. Deep navy and aged parchment palette evoking Lake Michigan's heritage
3. Generous whitespace punctuated by bold typographic anchors
4. Data-forward layout — numbers and statistics as visual heroes

**Color Philosophy:** Deep navy (#1B3A5C) as authority, warm sand (#F5E6C8) as approachability, copper accent (#B87333) for trust and precision. Evokes the Great Lakes maritime tradition without being kitschy.

**Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric split-column layout — left rail carries navigation and contact info persistently; right 70% holds content. Service area cards arranged like a regional map grid.

**Signature Elements:**
- Subtle topographic contour lines as section dividers
- Coordinate-style numbering (e.g., "42°N · 86°W") in service area headers
- Horizontal rule dividers styled as nautical chart depth lines

**Interaction Philosophy:** Calm, deliberate — hover states reveal depth (subtle card lift, color shift). No aggressive animations. Trust is built through stillness.

**Animation:** Slow fade-in on scroll (300ms ease-out). Form fields expand gently. No parallax.

**Typography System:** Playfair Display (headings, authority) + Source Sans Pro (body, clarity). Numbers in Roboto Mono for precision feel.

## Idea B: "Midwest Modernist — Clean Authority"

**Design Movement:** Swiss International Style adapted for professional services

**Core Principles:**
1. Strict typographic hierarchy — size and weight carry all visual weight
2. Off-white backgrounds with deep forest green and slate as anchors
3. Forms and CTAs treated as primary design elements, not afterthoughts
4. Photography bleeds edge-to-edge; text floats on semi-transparent overlays

**Color Philosophy:** Forest green (#2D5016) for trust and Michigan's natural landscape, warm off-white (#FAF8F5) for cleanliness, charcoal (#2C2C2C) for authority. Gold (#C9A84C) as a single accent for CTAs only.

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-bleed hero with left-aligned text. Content sections alternate between full-width and contained 3-column grids. Service area pages use a sidebar navigation pattern.

**Signature Elements:**
- Thick left-border accent lines on section headers (4px forest green)
- Photography with subtle green color overlay at 15% opacity
- Oversized section numbers (01, 02, 03) as structural waypoints

**Interaction Philosophy:** Direct and efficient — users should find what they need in 2 clicks. Sticky header with phone number always visible.

**Animation:** Slide-in from left on scroll for headings. Form submission triggers a smooth success state. Subtle button pulse on CTAs.

**Typography System:** Merriweather (headings, editorial gravitas) + Lato (body, approachable). All-caps small labels in letter-spaced Lato for section markers.

## Idea C: "Lakeshore Warmth — Residential Trust"

**Design Movement:** Contemporary residential real estate meets editorial warmth

**Core Principles:**
1. Warm, inviting palette that feels like a Michigan autumn — not cold corporate
2. Photography-first design — homes and landscapes carry emotional weight
3. Conversational copy style reflected in rounded, approachable UI elements
4. Micro-interactions that reward curiosity (hover reveals, accordion FAQs)

**Color Philosophy:** Warm slate blue (#3D5A80) as primary (lake water), terracotta (#C1440E) as accent (Michigan brick), cream (#FEFAF4) as background. Feels like a trusted neighbor, not a faceless corporation.

**Layout Paradigm:** Magazine-style editorial grid — hero takes 60% viewport height, then alternating full-bleed and card-grid sections. Service areas displayed as illustrated region cards.

**Signature Elements:**
- Rounded rectangle cards with soft drop shadows (not flat)
- Illustrated Michigan map SVG showing service areas with hover states
- Warm gradient overlays on hero images (slate-to-transparent)

**Interaction Philosophy:** Warm and guiding — tooltips explain appraisal types, FAQ accordions answer common questions, forms have inline validation with encouraging messages.

**Animation:** Staggered card entrance animations. Hero text types in on load (subtle). Smooth accordion transitions.

**Typography System:** Fraunces (display headings, warmth + character) + DM Sans (body, modern clarity). Service area names in bold DM Sans with terracotta underlines.

## Selected Approach: **Idea B — Midwest Modernist**

Chosen for its direct alignment with SEO goals (clear hierarchy = clear crawlability), professional authority (critical for appraisal credibility), and conversion-focused layout that treats forms as primary design elements. The forest green + off-white palette is distinctive without being distracting.
